A little about Papa Pete

Papa Pete opened his first pizzaria in 1955 in Pitsburg Pennsylvania. It was a family-run operation. He recruited the help of his three sons Chuck, Mike and Bob as the chefs after they had successfully mastered the family recipe. Pete's only daughter, Patty, worked spread the word of their restaurant throughout the city. Papa Pete's became the go-to place for quality pizza for all of Pitsburg.

Tragically, the mom and pop pizza shop burned down in 1960. Luckily, no one was hurt. However, there is a stirring family debate on if it was Bob or Mike that caused the fire. It seems the mystery may go unsolved.

It took the family a few years, but in the summer of 1963 they reopened their doors. Papa Pete's Pizza made national headlines as the "come-back business of the decade" with record sales in not only pizza but also merchandise. The memorable Papa Pete logo made it's mark on a generation of pizza enthusiasts.
